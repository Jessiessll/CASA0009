Flows are strongly clustered in the city center, which has numerous commercial and entertainment areas.
There is also a small peak at the University of Chicago station, probably due to the higher density of visitors.
There are 296 stations with greater inflows (attractiveness) and 229 stations with greater outflows (producticity).

When flows in stations are high, the number of people with high wages is high. While people with lower wages are usually with medium flows on the outskirts.

Comparing with start wage and end wage maps, the end wage map is more scattered, especially for those with medium and low wages.

Advice for...
Government
  • Conduct initiations to encourage enterprise to put bicycles in the suburbs.


  • Improve cycle lanes and regulate traffic rules, especially on routes with high use of shared bicycles.


  • Control the price of sharing bikes.

Companies

  • Put more bikes on the suburb area.


  • Put more bikes in area with high employment density, especially for service and office industry.


  • Set different price in different areas. For some areas with low income people gathering, the price could be lower with some conditions.

NGOs and Others

  • Propoganda to med/low income people, cars owners, people who are in industrial, entertainment and retail industries.


  • Actively support to governments about policy making of bike sharing.


  • Researchers could pay more attention on research considering socio economic factors based on above results.
